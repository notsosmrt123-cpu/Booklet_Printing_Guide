I. Initial Document Setup: The Foundation

This is the most critical phase. Getting the setup right from the start will save you significant time and prevent formatting issues later. Follow these steps in order to properly configure your document for booklet printing.

A. Switch to Booklet Layout ("Book fold")

On Windows: Navigate to the `Layout` tab, click the small arrow in the corner of the `Page Setup` group.

On macOS: Click `File` in the menu bar, then select `Page Setup`.

In the dialog, find `Multiple pages` and select **Book fold**. The orientation will automatically become *Landscape*, and margins will change to *Inside/Outside*.

B. Set Your Paper Size

In the `Page Setup` dialog box, go to the `Paper` tab. Select the paper size you will print on. Remember, this should be twice the size of your final booklet pages (e.g., use **Letter** paper for a 5.5" x 8.5" booklet).

C. Establish Your Margins

Return to the `Margins` tab to set your page margins. A good starting point is to have symmetrical margins for a clean look. See the table below for recommendations.

Margin Type Recommended Size
Top 0.5" to 0.75"
Bottom 0.5" to 0.75"
Inside 0.5" to 0.75" (before adding gutter)
Outside 0.5" to 0.75"

D. Configure the Crucial Gutter Margin

The gutter adds space to the inside margin for the binding. Thicker booklets need a larger gutter. Use the calculator below to find your recommended size.

II. Structuring and Formatting Your Content

With your document correctly set up, you can now focus on the content. Consistent formatting and proper structure are key to a professional-looking booklet.

A. Plan for a Page Count in Multiples of Four

Because one sheet of paper folded in half creates four booklet pages, your total page count **must be a multiple of 4**. If your content doesn't naturally fit (e.g., you have 21 pages), add blank pages at the end to reach the next multiple (e.g., 24).

B. Add Page Numbers and Headers/Footers

Go to the `Insert` tab and select `Page Number`. For a professional look, set up different headers/footers for odd and even pages to place page numbers on the outer corners. Enable this by checking **Different Odd & Even Pages** in the `Header & Footer` contextual tab.

C. Use Styles for Consistency

Use the **Styles** pane (`Home` tab) for all formatting (e.g., `Heading 1`, `Normal`). This allows you to update fonts or sizes globally by simply modifying the style, ensuring consistency throughout your document.

III. Final Review and Printing

Before you commit to printing, a final review and a test print are essential to catch any errors and save paper. This section guides you through the final steps for a perfect print.

A. Proofread and Preview

Go to `File > Print` to see the "printer spreads" (e.g., page 24 next to page 1). For the most accurate view, save a copy as a PDF. On macOS, the **"Open in Preview"** option from the print dialog is highly recommended for its accuracy.

B. Configure Printer Settings for Epson ET-15000

  1. Go to `File > Print` and select your **Epson ET-15000**.
  2. Click **Printer Properties**.
  3. On the **Main** tab, find the **2-Sided Printing** option.
  4. Select the **Booklet** setting.
  5. Ensure the binding is set to **Short-Edge Binding**. This is critical.
  6. Click **OK**.
  1. Go to `File > Print` and select your **Epson ET-15000**.
  2. Below the Orientation settings, find the dropdown menu (often says `Copies & Pages`).
  3. Select **Layout**.
  4. For **Two-Sided**, select **Short-Edge binding**.
  5. Proceed to print.

C. Perform a Test Print

Before printing the whole document, print just the first sheet. In Windows, type `p1,s1` in the Pages box. On macOS, use the "Open in Preview" method and print only the first page of the PDF. Fold this sheet to confirm that everything is correct.

IV. Troubleshooting Common Issues

Even with careful setup, you might run into issues. This section covers the most common problems and their solutions.

The back of my pages are printing upside down.

This is the most common issue. You must select **"Flip on short edge"** or **"Short-Edge Binding"** in your printer's properties. "Long-Edge Binding" is for portrait documents and will not work for booklets.

My margins are incorrect or look different when printed.

Some printer drivers override Word's settings. Check for options like "Scale to fit paper." Also, confirm you've selected the identical paper size in both Word's Page Setup and your Printer Properties.

The page order is completely wrong.

Go back to `Layout > Page Setup` and confirm that **"Book fold"** is still selected. If this setting is changed, Word will not impose the pages correctly.

I have extra blank pages at the end.

This is expected if your page count isn't a multiple of 4. Word automatically adds blank pages to complete the final sheet for printing.

V. After Printing: Assembling and Binding Your Booklet

The final step is to turn your printed sheets into a finished booklet. Proper folding and binding will give your project a professional touch.

A. Collate and Fold

Stack the printed sheets in the order they came out of the printer. Carefully fold the entire stack in half. For a crisp fold, press along the crease with a bone folder or the edge of a ruler.

B. Bind the Spine (Saddle Stitching)

Saddle stitching is the standard method, which means stapling along the center fold. Open the booklet and lay it flat, cover facing down. Use a **long-reach stapler** to place two or three staples along the fold.

**No long-reach stapler?** Open a regular stapler flat. Place the booklet spine over a block of foam or an eraser. Press to push the staple through, then manually fold the staple legs flat.
